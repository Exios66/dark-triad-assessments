The Dark Triad: Historical Background and Development

The Dark Triad is a constellation of three conceptually distinct but empirically overlapping personality traits: Machiavellianism, Narcissism, and Psychopathy.

Machiavellianism

Named after NiccolÃ² Machiavelli, a 16th-century Italian diplomat. The concept was introduced to psychology by Richard Christie and Florence Geis in the 1970s.

Narcissism

Derived from the Greek myth of Narcissus. It was introduced as a psychological concept by Havelock Ellis (1898) and developed by Sigmund Freud.

Psychopathy

Term coined by German psychiatrist Julius Koch in the 1890s. Hervey Cleckley's "The Mask of Sanity" (1941) provided the first comprehensive description.

The Dark Triad Concept

Introduced by Delroy L. Paulhus and Kevin M. Williams in 2002 to study these three traits together due to their conceptual distinctiveness but empirical overlap.

Key Developments

  • 2002: Introduction of the Dark Triad concept
  • 2010: Development of the Short Dark Triad (SD3)
  • 2010: Introduction of the Dirty Dozen scale
  • 2014: Expansion to the Dark Tetrad with the inclusion of everyday sadism
